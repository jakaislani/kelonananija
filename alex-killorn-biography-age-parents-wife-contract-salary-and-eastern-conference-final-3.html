Alex Killorn Biography and Wiki

Alex Killorn is a Canadian professional ice hockey centre.  Currently, he is playing for the Tampa Bay Lightning of the National Hockey League (NHL). Alex was selected by the Lightning in the third round, 77th overall, of the 2007 NHL Entry Draft.

Alex was named to Canada’s men’s national ice hockey team for the 2017 IIHF World Championship on April 19, 2017.  He earned a silver medal with Team Canada when they were defeated by Team Sweden 2-1 on May 21, 2017.

Alex Killorn Eastern Conference Final

Tampa Bay Lightning forward Alex was ejected from Game 2 of the Eastern Conference Final on September 9 2020 night boarding New York Islanders forward Brock Nelson. The incident happened just five minutes into the first period and resulted in Nelson leaving the game due to injury.

Alex was given a five-minute major and game misconduct for the hit, and it is really not hard to see why. Nelson had already made a play with the puck and no longer had possession of it when Alex skated in from behind and delivered a needless and reckless hit.

The hit drove Nelson’s head into the glass and knocked him to the ice where he remained for several moments before leaving for the locker room.

Alex Killorn Contract

Alex signed a two-year, entry-level contract with the Tampa Bay Lightning on May 19, 2012, after completing his playing career with the Harvard Crimson men’s ice hockey team.

He recorded his first career NHL assist on a goal scored by Vincent Lecavalier, against the New York Rangers on February 10, 2013. One week later, on February 17, 2013, Alex scored his first goal against the Florida Panthers. The Lightning announced on June 19, 2014 that they had re-signed Killorn to a two-year contract extension.

Alex had a successful post season run with the Tampa Bay Lightning during the 2015 Stanley Cup playoffs, helping them reach the Stanley Cup Final against the Chicago Blackhawks. The Lightning ended up falling to the Blackhawks in six games. In spite of the loss, Alex had a strong post season for the team and also posted a number of records for former players from Harvard. Alex’s game one goal was the first goal scored by a Harvard alum in a Stanley Cup final.

Alex added a second goal in game five to become the first alum with multiple goals or assists in the final. His 2015 postseason scoring totals (9 goals, 9 assists, 18 points) are all single-post season records among the twelve Harvard alumni who have played in the NHL postseason. Killorn’s 10 goals over two post seasons is second only to Dominic Moore (11 goals) on the Harvard all-time list, which was achieved in only 30 playoff games to Moore’s 88.

Alex recorded his 100th career NHL point in a 6-3 Lightning win over the Boston Bruins on October 12, 2015.   He skated in his 200th career NHL game in a 0-2 Lightning loss to the St. Louis Blues on October 27, 2015.

Alex signed a seven-year, $31 million extension with the Lightning on July 17, 2016.  Last season Alex appeared in 81 games with the team, recording 14 goals and 40 points. He also skated in 17 Stanley Cup Playoff games last year, where he scored five goals and 13 points. Alex has spent his entire NHL career with the Lightning, scoring 53 goals and 138 points. Alex’s biggest impact has come during the playoffs, where he has recorded 33 points in 47 games.

The NHL Department of Player Safety fined Killorn $5,000 on November 3, 2017, for an incident that occurred during a game against the New York Rangers the previous night at Amalie Arena. Alex recorded his 100th career NHL goal in a 5-2 Lightning victory over the Philadelphia Flyers at Wells Fargo Center on February 19, 2019. On March 16, 2019, Alex recorded his first career NHL hat-trick against the Washington Capitals.
