What ethnicity is Saffron Burrows?

Publish date: 2024-06-30

English actress and model Saffron Domini Burrows were born on October 22, 1972. She has acted in movies including Circle of Friends, Wing Commander, Deep Blue Sea, Gangster No. 1, Enigma, Troy, Reign Over Me, and The Bank Job.

She played Lorraine Weller on Boston Legal, Dr Norah Skinner in My Own Worst Enemy, Detective Serena Stevens in Law & Order: Criminal Intent, and Victoria Hand on Marvel’s Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D. on television. On Mozart in the Jungle on Amazon Video, she played Cynthia Taylor, and in You on Netflix, she played Dottie Quinn.

What ethnicity is Saffron Burrows?

Saffron is a British American.

In 1993, Burrows debuted in a movie with a little part in Jim Sheridan’s In the Name of the Father.

Her breakthrough appearances as an ambitious young Irishwoman in Circle of Friends and Ngozi Onwurah’s Welcome II The Terrordome occurred in 1995.

She starred in several films, including Hotel de Love, Lovelife, Nevada, One Night Stand, and The Matchmaker, as well as Dennis Potter’s Karaoke, a 1996 BBC production. She starred as identical twins in The Loss of Sexual Innocence, an experimental movie directed by Mike Figgis, in 1999. One twin was raised in England, the other in Italy.
