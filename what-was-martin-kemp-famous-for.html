What was Martin Kemp famous for?

Publish date: 2024-07-29

Martin Kemp is an English musician and actor, best known as the bassist in the new wave band Spandau Ballet and for his role as Steve Owen in EastEnders.

Martin Kemp is the younger brother of Gary Kemp, who is also a member of Spandau Ballet and an actor. Martin Kemp has also directed films and appeared on television shows such as Celebrity Big Brother and Let It Shine.

Martin Kemp appeared in several Hollywood films, including Waxwork II: Lost in Time (1992) and Embrace of the Vampire (1994). He was the subject of This Is Your Life in 1999.

Martin Kemp appeared on a celebrity special of Who Wants to Be a Millionaire? in January 2008 with his brother Gary. Kemp starred in the short-lived crime drama series Family in 2003.

Martin Kemp hosted TV’s 50 Hardest Men for Sky1 in July 2008 and guest-starred as Mr. Burley in the BBC drama series Waterloo Road. Kemp hosted the first series of “Martin Kemp’s Murder Files”.

Martin Kemp was featured in one edition of a factual television series for Five, Dangerous Adventures For Boys, based on the best-selling book written by Conn and Hal Iggulden, The Dangerous Book for Boys.
